FortiBleed Leak Exposes Fortinet VPN Credentials for ~75,000 Devices

A newly discovered data leak dubbed "FortiBleed" has exposed plaintext admin passwords for approximately 75,000 Fortinet FortiGate firewall devices, confirmed by Kevin Beaumont and BleepingComputer. The data appears to come from device configuration exports and includes credentials, IP addresses, device types, revenue brackets, and countries — a format commonly used by eCrime groups selling initial access. Many sampled devices are on recent firmware patches, and most FortiGate Management Interfaces are exposed to the internet. Unlike the 2022 Belsen Group leak, most affected devices are still online and this dataset largely covers different devices. Organizations are urged to check exposure via Hudson Rock, rotate admin credentials immediately, enable MFA, and assume compromise if suspect logins are found.
